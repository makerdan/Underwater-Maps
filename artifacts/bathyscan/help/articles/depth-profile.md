# Depth Profile

A **depth profile** is a vertical cross-section between two points — the same kind of chart you would see on a paper navigation chart.

## Drawing a profile

1. In fly mode, right-click the first point (point **A**) and choose **Measure from here** from the context menu.
2. Move the crosshair to the second point (point **B**) and click.

A panel slides in along the bottom centre showing:

- A line chart of depth vs distance.
- Length, minimum depth, maximum depth, and the difference (Δ).
- A thin coloured strip under the chart that shows the AI zone classification at each sample.
- Coordinates of both endpoints.

![Depth profile panel](/help/depth-profile.png)

## Reading the chart

- The Y-axis is depth (positive down — deeper is lower on the chart).
- The X-axis is distance from A to B.
- The cyan polyline is the actual seafloor.
- The faint gradient underneath is the area under the curve, for a sense of volume.

## Closing the profile

Click the **×** in the top-right of the profile panel to dismiss it.

## When to use this

- Planning a transect run for a real boat.
- Checking whether a route crosses a hazard.
- Comparing slope steepness between two regions.
- Quickly sanity-checking a depth claim from a chart.
